Revenue Optimization Case Study
Overview
This project analyzes 12 months of real healthcare business revenue data to identify growth opportunities and develop data-driven strategy recommendations.
Key Result: Identified £17,000-£34,000 in annual revenue opportunity through data-driven analysis.

The Problem
A healthcare clinic's monthly revenue fluctuates dramatically:

Best Month: £15,299 (March)
Worst Month: £5,590 (September)
Variation: 172% difference

Is this unavoidable seasonality or an addressable inefficiency?

Solution Approach
Using Python data analysis, statistical correlation, and business intelligence, I investigated:

What revenue patterns exist (seasonality analysis)
Why patterns occur (root cause investigation)
How to address them (growth strategy)


Key Findings
Finding #1: Severe Seasonal Fluctuation

Q4 (Jan-Mar): 34% of annual revenue
Q2 (Jul-Sep): 19% of annual revenue
Gap: £16,892 potential in Q2 alone

Finding #2: Root Causes Identified

July-September: Summer holidays, school breaks, reduced marketing
January-March: Spring sports season, New Year motivation, peak demand

Finding #3: Growth Opportunities
Three actionable strategies identified:

Summer Campaign Blitz: Target athletes + holiday prevention messaging
Extended Peak Season: Stretch Jan-Mar performance into Apr-Jun
Patient Retention Program: Convert one-time patients to recurring

Analysis Techniques

Key Insights
Insight #1: Marketing Timing Matters

February marketing campaigns correlate with the March peak
Summer marketing gaps correlate with September lows

Insight #2: Seasonality is Predictable

School holiday dates strongly correlate with revenue dips
Sports season timing correlates with revenue peaks

Insight #3: Patient Retention is Critical

Repeat patients provide a stable baseline revenue
New patient acquisition drives upside
